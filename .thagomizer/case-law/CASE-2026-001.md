Stateless object singletons fail the behavioral contract. AC1 enforcement is satisfied implicitly via AC2 dispatch function exhaustive when expression.

## Critic Review

The LOOP_BACK on Charge 1 is correct. The Judge's analysis of "yield" as a transitive verb requiring a direct object is sound — the spec text and background ("successful completion with a produced resource") together establish that Success must carry the resource, not merely signal its existence.

Three process observations for future advocates:

**1. The LOOP_BACK will likely surface a SPEC_AMBIGUITY.** The Judge's rationale correctly identifies that the next iteration must verify payload access — but the spec does not define the types of these payloads. X-23 will need to determine: what type is the "produced resource"? What type is the "retry path"? What type is the "fatal error signal"? If the story cannot answer these, iteration 2 will halt at SPEC_AMBIGUITY. Future prosecutors and defenders should anticipate this.

**2. The defender should have conceded payload and argued SPEC_AMBIGUITY from the start.** The correct defense was: "We agree the variants should carry payloads. But the spec does not define payload types. This is SPEC_AMBIGUITY, not IMPLEMENTATION_VIOLATION. Sustaining Charge 1 as IMPLEMENTATION_VIOLATION will produce another iteration that immediately hits SPEC_AMBIGUITY." This would have been more operationally useful than defending stateless singletons as sufficient.

**3. The Judge independently identified the strongest argument on Charge 2** — that the AC2 dispatch function's exhaustive when-without-else implicitly verifies the sealed nature of VulkanOutcome. Neither advocate surfaced this. Future defenders should identify implicit enforcement mechanisms early and make them explicit, rather than relying on judges to discover them.
